# Style: True Crime

## Overview
True crime style balances journalistic credibility with cinematic tension. It uses real assets (crime scene photos where appropriate, court documents, news clippings) mixed with moody graphic design and a score that builds dread. Pacing is deliberate — slow reveals, long holds, sudden cuts.

---

## Known Techniques (to be detailed via interview)

- **Redacted document zoom** — push into a real or styled document with text blacked out for dramatic effect
- **Archival photo reveal** — photos that fade or slide in, sometimes with a slight vignette or grain overlay
- **Location establishing shots** — real or stylized footage of relevant places (cities, crime scenes, courthouses)
- **Timeline reconstruction** — animated timeline that marks key events chronologically
- **Name / date cards** — clean text overlays identifying people, dates, and locations
- **Newspaper headline montage** — rapid cuts through period news coverage
- **Evidence board aesthetic** — photos and documents "pinned" to a corkboard with strings connecting them
- **Color grade** — desaturated, cool shadows, slight film grain overlay
- **Tension sound design** — low drone, stingers, silence before a cut
- **Reenactment / AI B-roll** — stylized footage (silhouettes, shadows) for scenes with no real footage
