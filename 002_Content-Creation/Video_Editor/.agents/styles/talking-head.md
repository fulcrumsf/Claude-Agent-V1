# Style: Talking Head

## Overview
The talking head style centers a speaker — host, expert, or interview subject — as the primary visual anchor. The challenge is keeping it visually dynamic through B-roll cutaways, motion graphic inserts, and smart lower-thirds. Used for YouTube educational content, explainers, and interview-driven documentaries.

---

## Known Techniques (to be detailed via interview)

- **Lower-thirds** — animated name / title cards that slide or fade in beneath the speaker
- **B-roll cutaway** — footage that illustrates what the speaker is describing
- **Text callout overlay** — key phrases or stats that appear on screen as the speaker says them
- **Chapter / section cards** — full-frame title cards that separate major segments
- **On-screen annotation** — arrows, circles, or highlights pointing to elements in the frame
- **Picture-in-picture** — secondary footage or screen recording inset while speaker continues
- **Clean transitions** — simple cross-dissolves or L-cuts between speaker and B-roll
- **Zoom punch-in** — subtle digital zoom on the speaker for emphasis
- **Background blur / bokeh** — soft focus on background to keep attention on speaker
