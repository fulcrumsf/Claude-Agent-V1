# Style: Nature Documentary

## Overview
The nature documentary aesthetic is cinematic and immersive — wide establishing shots, intimate close-ups, dramatic slow motion, and a score that builds tension and wonder. Graphic overlays are minimal and clean. The footage does the heavy lifting.

---

## Known Techniques (to be detailed via interview)

- **Establishing shot → close-up sequence** — wide landscape, cut to subject detail
- **Slow motion inserts** — high-speed footage of movement (wings, water, impact)
- **Lower-thirds / species callouts** — clean animated name cards (common + scientific name)
- **Location cards** — geographic text overlay with subtle animation
- **Atmospheric music scoring** — orchestral or ambient underscore that follows the narrative arc
- **Color grading** — warm golden hour tones, deep shadow contrast, vibrant greens
- **Sound design** — layered natural ambient sound under narration
- **Map inserts** — simple animated range maps or migration paths
- **Time-lapse / hyperlapse** — environmental transitions (day to night, season change)
