# Bioluminescence Research Fact Sheet
## Compiled for: Anomalous Wild — "The Animal That Makes Its Own Light"

### KEY CORRECTIONS TO SCRIPT
- Anglerfish bacteria: Aliivibrio sp. (NOT Photobacterium mandapamensis — outdated name)
- Deep-sea animal bioluminescence rate: ~90% (not 75%) — 75% is conservative/all-life estimate
- Dragonfish red-light fact: Malacosteus niger CANNOT genetically produce red-light sensitivity — it ACQUIRES it by eating copepods that ate bacteria containing bacteriochlorophyll derivatives. The pigment incorporates into its retina. Change its diet → change its vision.
- Cookiecutter shark dark throat collar = fake small fish silhouette → draws in tuna/dolphins/whale sharks → bites circular plug. US Navy documented bite marks on nuclear submarine sonar domes.
- Milky Sea events: dinoflagellate blooms visible from SPACE — 100,000 km² patch photographed by satellite (2019 VIIRS imagery)
- GFP (Green Fluorescent Protein) from jellyfish Aequorea victoria → 2008 Nobel Prize in Chemistry → used to make cancer cells glow, track viruses, watch neurons fire. Bioluminescence literally changed medicine.
- Largest bioluminescent vertebrate: Kitefin shark (Dalatias licha, ~180cm) — documented 2021 Frontiers in Marine Science
- Siphonophore genus Erenna: bioluminescent tentacle tips that MIMIC copepods/crustaceans in red-pink light — active lure deployed by a 40m colonial predator

- Luciferase + luciferin terminology: functional names, not specific molecules — at least 9 chemically distinct luciferins identified

- Cookiecutter photophores: Claes et al. 2010 PLOS ONE — 1M+ photocytes
- Bobtail squid symbiosis: McFall-Ngai & Ruby 1991 Cell — landmark paper
