# POLYOCULIS — CHANNEL CONTENT SYSTEM

## CORE IDENTITY

- **Brand:** Polyoculis
- **Tagline:** Strange facts. Stranger worlds. Explained.
- **Tone:** Curious, precise, stylized — Vox meets "Fern" — curiosity-hook baiting with a very specific visual aesthetic
- **Audience:** Curious generalists, explainer video fans, geography/natural phenomena enthusiasts, true crime adjacent, ages 16–35
- **Concept:** Explainer-driven channel covering geography, natural phenomena, mysteries of ancient structures, true crime breakdowns, and bank heists — told through a distinctive 3D animation style: smooth, minimal-faced human figures in realistic environments. The figures look like polished corporate mannequins — present but slightly uncanny. Not silhouettes, not cartoon — a clinical 3D style that makes everyday situations feel subtly wrong. Think Vox-quality scripting with a visual aesthetic that's just slightly off from reality.

---

## VISUAL IDENTITY

### Animation Style — Reference: Screenshot provided
- **3D rendered human figures** — smooth, bald or minimal-hair, simplified facial features (eyes, brows present but stylized) — the "slightly off" quality is intentional
- Figures wear normal clothing (suits, casual wear) — the strangeness is in how normal they look while being not-quite-right
- **Realistic environments** — office rooms, meeting tables, urban settings, natural environments — rendered with real-world lighting and depth
- The contrast between realistic space and stylized figures creates the channel's signature uncanny register
- Motion design: confident, purposeful — camera moves, cuts with editorial intent
- Text overlays: bold, well-spaced, integrated — not decorative, functional
- Data visualization: maps, diagrams, timelines rendered in the same 3D visual space when possible

### Color Palette
- **Deep Slate:** `#1A1F2E` (background — the color thinking happens in)
- **Electric Blue:** `#2B7BFF` (data, maps, callouts — primary accent)
- **Warm Amber:** `#E8A024` (human interest, contrast accent)
- **Off-White:** `#F0EDE8` (text, negative space)
- **Muted Red:** `#C0392B` (crime, danger, tension moments)

### Palette Vibe
Vox/NYT explainer meets "Fern" silhouette aesthetic. Serious visual design applied to fascinating topics. The production quality signals: this is worth your time.

## CONTENT PILLARS

1. **Geography deep-dives** — why a country is shaped the way it is, how a river changed a civilization, the geography of conflict
2. **Natural phenomena** — ball lightning, rogue waves, the Bermuda Triangle, the Door to Hell — explained rigorously but dramatically
3. **Ancient mysteries** — the pyramids, Stonehenge, Nazca lines — not conspiracy, but the genuinely unanswered engineering and archaeological questions
4. **True crime explainers** — bank heists, elaborate frauds, disappearances — structured like a heist movie, told in the silhouette style
5. **Strange facts and records** — things that are technically true that most people don't know and can't stop thinking about once they do

### What Makes Polyoculis Different
Every major explainer channel uses talking heads or flat motion graphics. Polyoculis uses the Fern silhouette 3D aesthetic — it's immediately visually distinct. The combination of Vox-quality research + "Fern"-style visuals in topics beyond film/media is the gap this channel occupies.

## THUMBNAIL SYSTEM

### Style
- Dark slate background with one strong visual element — often a stylized 3D silhouette or a bold graphic
- Electric blue and amber as accent colors
- Bold, clean sans-serif text — large, direct, no decorative fonts
- Map or location graphic common for geography content
- Crime/heist content: the silhouette figure in motion — caught mid-action

### Rules
**DO:**
- Use the channel's silhouette aesthetic in thumbnails where possible
- Bold, specific text — "The Heist That Broke the Federal Reserve" not "Amazing Bank Robbery"
- Map/geography thumbnails: the shape itself is often the hook
- High contrast — designed for small screen

**DON'T:**
- No talking-head thumbnails
- No generic stock footage
- Don't be vague — every thumbnail should communicate the specific topic

## TITLE SYSTEM

### Format
- "[The Specific Thing] — Explained"
- Or: "Why [Geographic/Scientific Phenomenon] Happens"
- Or: "The [Heist/Crime/Disappearance] That [Record-Breaking Claim]"
- Or: "[Fascinating Specific Fact] (Most People Don't Know This)"

### Examples
- "The Heist That Stole $1.2 Billion in Plain Sight — Explained"
- "Why the Nile Flows North (And What That Changed)"
- "The Pyramid Engineering Question Nobody Has Answered"
- "Ball Lightning: What It Is and Why We Can't Replicate It"
- "The Country That Technically Doesn't Exist — But Does"
- "The Biggest Bank Robbery in History — How They Actually Did It"

### Title Patterns That Work
- `The [Specific Event/Phenomenon] — Explained`
- `Why [Geography/Science Claim] (That You Never Thought About)`
- `The [Heist/Crime/Disappearance] That [Record or Hook]`
- `[Fascinating True Claim] Most People Don't Know`

## HOOK SYSTEM

### First 3 Seconds
- Bold visual statement — the map, the silhouette mid-action, the phenomenon starting
- Or: a stat that reframes scale: "This bank robbery took 4 hours. They were never caught. And the money was never found."

### First 10 Seconds
- State the topic and the hook: what it is, why it's more interesting than it sounds
- Show 2–3 quick visuals that signal production quality — confident pace
- End the intro with the question or mystery that drives the full video
