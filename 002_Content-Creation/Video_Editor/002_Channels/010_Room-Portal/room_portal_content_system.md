# ROOM PORTAL — CHANNEL CONTENT SYSTEM

## CORE IDENTITY

- **Brand:** Room Portal
- **Tagline:** Step inside. Study. Stay a while.
- **Tone:** Calm, wordless, atmospheric — content speaks entirely through visuals and sound
- **Audience:** Students, remote workers, lo-fi music listeners, Ghibli fans, cozy aesthetic enthusiasts, ages 14–35
- **Concept:** Immersive lo-fi audio + Ghibli-style animated visual loops designed for study, focus, and ambient projection. The goal is to make viewers feel like they're *inside* an anime scene — studying in a snow cabin, working in a Japanese café during rain, sitting in a cozy Ghibli-world house. Designed for screen watching and wall/ceiling projector use.

---

## VISUAL IDENTITY

### Animation Style
- **Ghibli-inspired animated loops** — not exact replicas, but the same warmth and hand-drawn quality
- Cozy interior spaces: wood-beamed cabins, lantern-lit rooms, rain-streaked windows, bookshelf walls
- Exterior scenes: snow falling outside a warm window, moonlit forests, Japanese village streets at night
- Gentle motion loops: snow drifting, steam rising from tea, curtains moving slightly, leaves falling
- NO characters speaking, NO people with faces — implied presence only (a book, a teacup, a lit candle)

### Projection-First Design
- Wide format (16:9 or wider) — optimized for projector output
- High visual fidelity — viewers will project this full-wall at home
- Seamlessly loopable — no visible transition point
- Dark-room compatible — rich blacks, warm practical lighting sources (fire, lanterns, desk lamps)

---

## COLOR SYSTEM

- **Warm Amber:** `#D4874A` (firelight, paper lanterns, sunset through windows)
- **Soft Indigo:** `#2E3A6B` (night sky, rain, evening study atmosphere)
- **Cream/Candlelight:** `#FFF3D4` (lit interior surfaces, warm glow)
- **Forest Green:** `#3A5A40` (foliage, outdoor nature scenes)
- **Deep Charcoal:** `#1A1A2E` (shadow, night backgrounds)

### Palette Vibe
Studio Ghibli color logic — each scene has a dominant warm/cool contrast. Firelight vs. cold window. Warm desk lamp vs. blue night outside. Every frame should feel like a still from a film you want to live inside.

---

## AUDIO IDENTITY

### Music Style
- Lo-fi hip-hop, lo-fi jazz, or ambient instrumental
- Slow tempo (70–90 BPM) — study-optimized
- Soft vinyl crackle or rain layer optional but on-brand
- NO vocals, NO lyrics
- Mood: focused, warm, slightly melancholic — the Ghibli emotional register

### Audio Distribution Goals
- YouTube (primary)
- Spotify and Apple Music (future) — release audio as lo-fi playlists
- Note: AI-generated music is viable as long as platform policies allow; monitor policy developments

## CONTENT PILLARS

1. **Study cabin — snow** — cozy wooden cabin, snow falling outside, fireplace crackling, warm desk lamp
2. **Japanese rainy evening** — wood-and-paper architecture, rain on roof, steam rising from noodles
3. **Bookshop / library** — floor-to-ceiling shelves, afternoon light through old windows, distant street sounds
4. **Treehouse / forest** — wooden walls, leaves rustling, birds in the canopy, morning light
5. **Rooftop urban night** — city lights below, open sky, warm tea, gentle wind

### Duration Format
- 1-hour versions (standard, YouTube searchable)
- 3-hour versions (study session length)
- 8–10-hour versions (sleep / all-night study / ambient background)
- Each environment = one upload per duration (ecosystem of the same scene at different lengths)

## THUMBNAIL SYSTEM

### Style
- A beautiful, composed still from the animation loop — warmly lit, the most "I want to be there" frame
- Minimal or no text — the environment sells itself
- If text: small, calm, bottom-center — "[Environment] | [Duration] | Lo-Fi Study"
- Soft vignette edges — cinematic framing

### Rules
**DO:**
- Choose the warmest, most inviting frame — firelight, rain on glass, snow outside a lit window
- Consistent framing: interior with warm light source and cold/dark exterior
- Ghibli color palette throughout

**DON'T:**
- No loud text overlays
- No thumbnails that look like stock photography
- No hard-edged graphics or modern UI aesthetics

## HOOK SYSTEM

The hook is the first frame. No narration. No intro.

First 3 seconds:
- Scene fully established — snow falling, fire crackling, steam rising
- Audio mix present — music, ambient layer locked in
- Viewer should feel: "I want to stay here"
