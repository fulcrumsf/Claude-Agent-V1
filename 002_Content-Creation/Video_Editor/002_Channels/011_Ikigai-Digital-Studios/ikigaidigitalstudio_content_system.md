# IKIGAI DIGITAL STUDIO — CHANNEL CONTENT SYSTEM

## CORE IDENTITY

- **Brand:** IkigaiDigitalStudio
- **Tagline:** Beautiful printable art — and how to bring it to life.
- **Tone:** Warm, helpful, artisan — a maker showing you how to make the most of beautiful things
- **Audience:** Home decorators, printable art buyers, DIY print-at-home enthusiasts, Etsy shoppers, ages 25–55
- **Concept:** YouTube channel as marketing and support arm for the Ikigai Digital Studio brand — an Etsy store (and eventually own-website) selling digital downloadable wall art. Videos showcase the artwork, teach customers how to print and frame it correctly, and build trust that drives store sales. The channel bridges discovery and purchase intent.

## BUSINESS CONTEXT

- **Primary Store:** Ikigai Digital Studio on Etsy (digital downloadable wall art)
- **Future Store:** Own website — likely LemonSqueezy or similar (to own customer data, avoid Etsy fees)
- **Revenue Model:** Sell digital art files (no inventory, no shipping) — customer downloads and prints
- **Channel Role:** Top-of-funnel discovery → product trust → purchase → post-purchase support

## CONTENT PILLARS

1. **Print sizing tutorials** — how to resize a file to 5×7, 8×10, 11×14, A4, A3 — in Photoshop, Photopea, and basic phone/computer tools
2. **Printer quality comparisons** — testing Ikigai art prints on different home printers and paper types (matte, semi-gloss, fine art)
3. **Framing tutorials** — how to crop to fit standard frame sizes, how to mat prints, how to hang a gallery wall
4. **Artwork showcases** — showing the art in real home environments — styled photos, room mockups
5. **Store news and drops** — new collections, seasonal art drops, how to use digital downloads from the store

### Core SEO Strategy
Every tutorial video should answer a question a buyer has AFTER purchasing: "How do I print this at 8×10?" "What paper should I use?" "How do I resize to fit my IKEA frame?" These videos support the sale AND drive new discovery from people who searched the same question.

## THUMBNAIL SYSTEM

### Style
- Warm, home-interior aesthetic — not software/tech look
- Central visual: the finished print in a real frame on a wall, OR a before/after print comparison
- Text: clean, readable, helpful — "How to Print 8×10 at Home" — not clickbait
- Colors: warm cream, terracotta, gold — matches store aesthetic

### Layout
- Finished art in context (framed, hung) as the primary visual draw
- Print quality comparison: side-by-side close-up shots
- Tutorial thumbnails: step-indicator or result image + tool logo if needed

### Rules
**DO:**
- Show the final print in a beautiful home setting — sell the outcome
- Use warm color grading consistent with store palette
- Include the store name or watermark subtly

**DON'T:**
- Don't make it look like a tech tutorial channel — it's a home décor channel
- No generic stock interiors — use actual prints from the store
- Don't hide what the video is — clarity first

## HOOK SYSTEM

### First 3 Seconds
- Show a beautiful finished print in a real room — "this is what it looks like printed"
- Or: state the exact problem being solved: "If your print came out the wrong size for your frame, here's exactly how to fix it"

### First 10 Seconds
- Show the output (finished, framed art) immediately
- Brief context: "I sell printable wall art and I get this question constantly — so here's the full answer"
- Establish credibility: you're the maker, you know your product
