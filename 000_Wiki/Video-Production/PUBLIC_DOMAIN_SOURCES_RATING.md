## Summary

A rated and tiered prioritization guide for sourcing public-domain and Creative Commons-licensed video, image, and audio assets in video production pipelines. Each source is scored 1-10 against broadcast-quality production needs, with use-case fit and a recommendation tier.

Tier 1 ("always query first," 8-9/10) covers NASA for space/Earth science, Pexels for contemporary stock, Wikimedia Commons for archival/historical, Library of Congress for museum-quality historical, and Pixabay as a Pexels alternative. Tier 2 (7/10) includes Openverse for audio, Internet Archive for vintage footage, and Europeana for cultural heritage. Tier 3 (6/10) holds niche sources like Chronicling America, Trove (Australian), Coverr, and David Rumsey Maps. GBIF is rated lowest (5/10) due to unreliable licensing metadata.

The document operationalizes which sources researchers should hit first for each asset type, reducing time spent on poor-fit archives and ensuring licensing clarity for commercial video production.
