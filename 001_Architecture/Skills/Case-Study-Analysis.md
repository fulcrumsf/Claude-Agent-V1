# Case Study Analysis Framework

## Purpose
Systematically analyze video performance and extract learnings for future content. Case studies document what works, why it works, and quantifiable results.

## Framework Structure

### 1. Video Metadata
- **Video Title:** Exact title as published
- **URL:** Permanent link
- **Channel:** Which brand/channel
- **Publish Date:** When released
- **Duration:** Video length
- **Format:** Documentary, tutorial, shorts, etc.
- **Cinematic Style Used:** Reference from Cinematic-Styles.md
- **Budget Range:** Estimated production cost

### 2. Performance Metrics
- **View Count:** Total views (snapshot date)
- **Watch Time:** Total minutes watched
- **Click-Through Rate:** If applicable
- **Engagement Rate:** Likes, comments, shares as % of views
- **Revenue Generated:** AdSense, affiliate, sponsorship (if available)
- **Growth Impact:** New subscribers gained
- **Viral Score:** (Engagements per 1000 views) — benchmark against channel average

### 3. Content Analysis

#### Hook & Title Strategy
- Title breakdown: Is it curiosity gap, number, question, statement?
- Hook type: Visual, narrative, question, surprise
- Why it works for this audience

#### Narrative Structure
- Opening (0–15 seconds): What's the hook?
- Rising action: How is tension/interest built?
- Climax/Payoff: When does the "aha" moment arrive?
- Resolution: How does it close?

#### Visual Elements
- Dominant cinematic style
- Color palette and grading
- Pacing (cuts per minute)
- Graphics/text overlay frequency
- Thumbnail elements used

#### Audio Strategy
- Music/soundtrack choice
- Voiceover tone and pacing
- Sound design moments
- Call-to-action audio cues

### 4. Audience-Specific Insights
- **Target Audience:** Who does this appeal to?
- **Why They Watched:** Inferred from comments, retention data
- **Engagement Sentiment:** Positive, mixed, controversial?
- **Comment Themes:** What are viewers discussing?

### 5. Learnings & Recommendations
- **Top 3 Replicable Elements:** What made this work?
- **What Didn't Work:** Segments with drop-off
- **A/B Test Opportunities:** What could be tested differently?
- **Franchisability:** Can this concept be repeated?
- **Cross-Platform Adaptation:** Works on TikTok/Instagram/Shorts?

### 6. Benchmarking
- **Channel Average Comparison:** How does this video perform vs. channel baseline?
- **Category Benchmark:** How does it compare to similar content?
- **Growth Trajectory:** Did views spike or grow steadily?

## Output Format

Case studies are stored as Markdown files in the brand folder:

```
/002_Brands/[Brand-Name]/Case-Studies/[Video-Title].md
```

Example:
```
/002_Brands/Anomalous-Wild/Case-Studies/what_they_found_in_the_deepest_place_on_earth.md
```

## When to Create

- After every major video release (threshold: >1,000 views or >5% engagement)
- When a video significantly outperforms channel average
- When testing a new strategy, format, or hook type
- When a video reveals surprising audience insights
- When content is ready for portfolio/pitch deck use

## When NOT to Create

- For every minor upload (keep focus on learning videos)
- Before 72 hours of performance data (give video time to settle)
- For platform-native shorts unless part of series analysis

## Review Cycle

- Review case study 1 week post-publication (initial performance)
- Update with 30-day data (long-tail view growth)
- Archive to reference-material for future template hunting

## Integration

Case studies are referenced in:
- Content planning (as templates for future videos)
- Brand guidelines (as proof of what resonates)
- Portfolio and pitch decks
- Cinematic Style decision trees (as real examples)
