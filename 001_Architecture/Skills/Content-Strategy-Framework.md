# Content Strategy Framework

## Purpose

This framework guides strategic planning for content calendars, audience segmentation, and revenue attribution across all brands and platforms.

## Core Principles

1. **Audience-First:** Every content decision starts with audience needs/interests
2. **Revenue Integration:** Every piece of content has a monetization pathway (direct, affiliate, funnel)
3. **Franchisability:** Successful content themes should be repeatable and scaleable
4. **Cross-Platform:** Content is designed to work across YouTube, shorts, social, and email
5. **Data-Driven:** Planning is informed by case studies and analytics, not guesses

## Framework Structure

### 1. Audience Definition

#### 1.1 Primary Audience Persona
- **Demographics:** Age, location, income, education
- **Psychographics:** Values, fears, aspirations, hobbies
- **Pain Points:** What problems does this audience face?
- **Desires:** What do they want to learn/achieve/experience?
- **Trust Triggers:** What makes them trust a creator?
- **Platform Preference:** YouTube, TikTok, Instagram, email?

#### 1.2 Audience Behavior
- **Peak Activity Times:** When do they consume content?
- **Content Preferences:** Long-form, shorts, entertainment, education?
- **Engagement Patterns:** Do they comment? Share? Subscribe?
- **Monetization Receptiveness:** Open to affiliate links? Ads? Digital products?
- **Channel Growth Rate:** How fast is this audience growing?

#### 1.3 Competitive Landscape
- **Top 3 Competitors:** Who else serves this audience?
- **Their Strengths:** What are they doing well?
- **Your Differentiation:** What's your unique angle?
- **Market Gap:** What are competitors NOT addressing?

### 2. Content Pillars & Themes

Organize content into 3–5 core themes/pillars that resonate with your audience:

**Example Pillars:**
- **Pillar 1:** Exploration & Discovery (e.g., "unusual places in nature")
- **Pillar 2:** Education & How-To (e.g., "science explained")
- **Pillar 3:** Personal Journey (e.g., "living nomadically")
- **Pillar 4:** Urgency & Trending (e.g., "new discoveries, viral stories")
- **Pillar 5:** Community & Culture (e.g., "viewer stories, Q&A")

**For Each Pillar:**
- Target audience segment
- Platform fit (YouTube long-form, shorts, TikTok, etc.)
- Content type (documentary, vlog, tutorial, storytelling)
- Cinematic style (ref: 000_Skills/Cinematic-Styles.md)
- Monetization pathway
- Example topics and case studies

### 3. Content Calendar Strategy

#### 3.1 Publication Frequency

- **Shorts:** [Define: 2–5x/week]
- **Social Platforms:** [Define: 5–7x/week]

Define percentage breakdown:

- [Pillar 2]: X% of monthly output

**Example:** 40% educational, 30% exploration, 20% trending, 10% community

#### 3.3 Quarterly Planning
- **Q[X] Theme:** What's the overarching narrative for the quarter?
- **Key Series:** What multi-part series will launch?
- **Hero Content:** Which 2–3 "big" videos will anchor the quarter?
- **Sponsor/Partnership Opportunities:** Any planned collaborations?
- **Promotion Plan:** How will you drive awareness?

#### 3.4 Monthly Breakdown
- Calendar view of all planned content
- Publishing dates locked in
- Topic assignments
- Resource allocation (editing, assets, promotion)
- Revenue goals for the month

### 4. Audience Segmentation Strategy

Not all audience members engage the same way. Segment and tailor:

#### 4.1 Segment Definition
- **Core Fans:** Subscribe, watch everything, high engagement
- **Casual Viewers:** Watch occasionally, may not subscribe
- **Affiliate Prospects:** Interested in product recommendations
- **Digital Product Buyers:** Likely to purchase guides, templates
- **Community Members:** Active commenters, participants
- **Lurkers:** Watch but don't engage publicly

#### 4.2 Content Tailoring by Segment
- **Core Fans:** Bonus behind-the-scenes, community polls, Q&A
- **Casual Viewers:** Best-performing content from other creators
- **Affiliate Prospects:** Product-aligned content with links
- **Digital Product Buyers:** Educational, solution-focused content
- **Community Members:** Discussion-prompting videos
- **Lurkers:** High-value story content that inspires sharing

#### 4.3 Platform Distribution by Segment
- **Email:** Core fans + digital product buyers
- **YouTube Community Tab:** Core fans, community members
- **TikTok/Shorts:** Casual viewers, new audience reach
- **Instagram:** Lifestyle/inspiration angle for aspirational segment
- **Discord/Community:** Core fans only

### 5. Monetization Framework

#### 5.1 Revenue Streams & Attribution
For each content pillar, identify revenue sources:

| Pillar | Ad Revenue | Affiliate | Sponsorship | Digital Product | Funnel Stage |
|--------|-----------|-----------|-------------|-----------------|--------------|
| [Pillar 1] | High | Medium | Possible | Low | Awareness |
| [Pillar 2] | Medium | High | Yes | High | Consideration |
| [Pillar 3] | Low | Low | No | Medium | Engagement |

#### 5.2 Affiliate Strategy
- **Affiliate Programs:** Which programs align with your audience?
  - Amazon Associates
  - Impact Network (travel, apps)
  - SaaS tools
  - Digital product platforms
- **Link Placement:** Where do affiliate links go? (description, pinned comment, timestamps)
- **Disclosure:** Always follow FTC guidelines
- **Performance Tracking:** Measure CTR and conversion by video/platform

#### 5.3 Sponsorship Strategy
- **Target Sponsors:** Which brands align with your audience?
- **Pitch Package:** What data do you show to sponsors?
- **Integration:** How do you naturally integrate sponsor content?
- **Pricing:** What's your rate card by view count?

#### 5.4 Digital Product Strategy
- **Product Types:** What do you sell? (guides, templates, courses)
- **Launch Timing:** When in content calendar do you promote?
- **Email Funnel:** How does video content feed your email list?
- **Upsell Moments:** Which content naturally leads to product?

### 6. Growth & Performance Targets

#### 6.1 Channel-Level Goals
- **Subscriber Growth:** Target % YoY
- **Total Views:** Monthly target
- **Watch Time:** Monthly target (hours)
- **Engagement Rate:** Target % above channel baseline
- **Revenue Target:** Monthly/quarterly goals by stream

#### 6.2 Pillar-Specific Goals
Each pillar has targets:
- Pillar 1: [500k views/month, 5% engagement, $X affiliate revenue]
- Pillar 2: [X new subscribers/month, Y digital product sales]
- etc.

#### 6.3 Video-Level Benchmarks
- **Minimum Performance:** When is a video considered "underperforming"?
- **Average Performance:** Historical baseline for channel
- **Success Threshold:** What metrics trigger a case study?
- **Viral Potential:** What 5 elements consistently drive high engagement?

### 7. Content Research & Inspiration

#### 7.1 Competitive Monitoring
- Track top performers in your niche
- Monitor comments for audience demand
- Watch trending content on platforms
- Note emerging tools/formats

#### 7.2 Audience Research
- Regular YouTube comments analysis
- Surveys or polls (email, community tab)
- Social listening for your brand
- Analytics deep-dives on high/low performers

#### 7.3 Inspiration Sources
- Obsidian Vault: 004_Research/reference-material/
- NotebookLM notebooks for factual topics
- Case study analysis (000_Skills/Case-Study-Analysis.md)
- Industry news and emerging trends

### 8. Workflow Integration

#### 8.1 Before Creating Content
1. Check content calendar
2. Identify audience segment(s)
3. Confirm pillar fit and monetization pathway
4. Research competitive landscape
5. Gather reference materials (research, case studies, NotebookLM)

#### 8.2 During Production
- Follow Video-Production-Workflow.md
- Select cinematic style based on audience & pillar
- Optimize for platform(s) in content calendar
- Integrate monetization links thoughtfully

#### 8.3 After Publishing
- Monitor analytics per segment
- Compare to pillar-specific goals
- Track revenue attribution
- Create case study if threshold met

## Review & Update Cycle

- **Monthly:** Review performance against targets, adjust next month's calendar
- **Quarterly:** Strategic review, content mix adjustment, goal setting
- **Annually:** Full audit, persona refresh, pillar evaluation, competitive landscape reassessment

## Success Metrics

You'll know this framework is working when:
- Content calendar is predictive (you publish, metrics match predictions)
- Audience feels known (comments reflect understood needs)
- Revenue compounds (monetization strategy generates increasing ROI)
- Case studies are frequent (high performers yield learnings)
- Team efficiency improves (less guessing, more execution)
